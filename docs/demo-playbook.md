# Atlas HR Demo Playbook

## 45-minute demo structure

1. Discovery, 10 minutes: current HR tools, painful workflows, employee count, countries, buyer, decision timeline.
2. Knowledge moat, 5 minutes: Knowledge Hub, country guide, Copilot answer for a real compliance question.
3. Tools, 10 minutes: show one or two generators tied to the buyer's pain, such as termination letters or policy drafts.
4. HRIS, 15 minutes: operational dashboard, employee profile, compliance center, approvals, employee self-service portal.
5. Pricing and next steps, 5 minutes: map them to Team, Business, or Enterprise pilot and confirm the next meeting.

## Pre-demo checklist

- Research the company website, LinkedIn, headcount range, countries, and likely compliance exposure.
- Create a sandbox workspace named after the company with five realistic sample employees.
- Prepare one country-specific compliance example and one workflow example relevant to their industry.
- Confirm calendar link, video link, and the contact's role in the buying process.

## Common objections

- "Why not BambooHR?" Atlas HR combines operational records with country-aware knowledge, Copilot guidance, and compliance context.
- "We're too small." Start with Team if they run daily HR ops, or Free/Pro if they are still solo and content-led.
- "We already use Workday." Position Atlas HR for the day-to-day HR workflows and local compliance work that heavyweight systems make slow.
- "AI is risky for HR." Explain permission scopes, audit logs, human approval, and the rule that Copilot cannot invent data.
- "We need SSO." SSO is Enterprise-scoped; collect identity provider, SAML/OIDC requirements, and timing.
- "We need payroll." Atlas HR does not run payroll; it stores workforce records and can later integrate with payroll systems.
- "Can we import data?" Yes for pilot onboarding; use templates first, then scoped API work once Phase F lands.
- "Can employees self-serve?" Yes, employees can manage profile data, documents, leave, tasks, and policy acknowledgments.
- "How long does implementation take?" Team can start in days; Enterprise pilots use a 90-day rollout with weekly check-ins.
- "What if the pilot fails?" Enterprise pilots include clear success metrics and a conversion decision at day 90.
