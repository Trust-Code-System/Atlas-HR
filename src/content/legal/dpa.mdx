## Definitions

This Data Processing Addendum applies when Atlas HR processes personal data on behalf of a Team or Enterprise customer. Controller, Processor, Personal Data, Data Subject, Processing, Subprocessor, and Supervisory Authority have the meanings given under applicable data protection law, including GDPR where applicable.

## Subject Matter and Duration

Atlas HR processes customer personal data to provide HR workspace, document, Copilot, Mini-HRIS, community, billing, support, and related services. Processing lasts for the subscription term and any retention period required by law or agreed deletion workflow.

## Nature and Purpose of Processing

Processing includes hosting, storing, transmitting, organising, retrieving, generating, analysing, securing, backing up, deleting, and supporting customer data. The purpose is to provide Atlas HR under the customer's instructions.

## Data Subjects

Data subjects may include customer administrators, employees, candidates, contractors, community users, invited teammates, HR contacts, and support contacts.

## Categories of Personal Data

Personal data may include names, emails, job titles, departments, countries, salaries, leave records, performance notes, onboarding records, policy acknowledgements, generated documents, support communications, usage logs, and account metadata.

## Atlas HR Obligations as Processor

Atlas HR will process personal data only on documented instructions, ensure confidentiality, implement reasonable security measures, assist with data subject requests where possible, support breach notification duties, maintain relevant records, and delete or return customer personal data at the end of services subject to legal retention.

## Customer Obligations as Controller

The customer is responsible for lawful basis, employee notices, data accuracy, role permissions, instructions, compliance decisions, and ensuring that data uploaded to Atlas HR may lawfully be processed.

## Security Measures

Atlas HR security measures include TLS, infrastructure encryption at rest where available, access controls, RBAC, service-role separation, webhook signing, audit-oriented logging, restricted production access, dependency updates, incident response processes, and periodic security review.

## Subprocessors

Atlas HR may use subprocessors including Supabase for database and authentication, Vercel for hosting, Stripe for payments and tax, Resend for email, Anthropic for AI processing, PostHog for analytics where enabled, and Sentry for error monitoring where enabled. Locations and processing details should be reviewed before production launch.

## International Transfers

Where customer personal data is transferred internationally, Atlas HR will rely on appropriate transfer mechanisms such as Standard Contractual Clauses, adequacy decisions, or other safeguards required by applicable law.

## Audits and Inspections

Upon reasonable written request, Atlas HR will provide information necessary to demonstrate compliance. Audits must be scoped, scheduled, confidential, and avoid compromising other customers or platform security.

## Liability

Liability under this DPA is subject to the limits in the Terms of Service unless applicable law requires otherwise.

## Term and Termination

This DPA remains in effect while Atlas HR processes customer personal data. Upon termination, Atlas HR will delete or return personal data according to product capabilities, retention obligations, and backup lifecycle.

## Annex I: Description of Processing

Atlas HR processes HR operations data to generate documents, manage team HR records, support AI-assisted workflows, deliver notifications, and operate subscription services.

## Annex II: Technical and Organisational Measures

Measures include encryption in transit, managed infrastructure controls, least-privilege access, authentication, role-based permissions, logging, backup practices, secure secret handling, incident response, and vendor review.

## Annex III: List of Subprocessors

Subprocessors include Supabase, Vercel, Stripe, Resend, Anthropic, PostHog, and Sentry. This list must be finalised before production launch.
