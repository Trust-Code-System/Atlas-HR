Atlas Copilot is a genuinely useful tool for HR professionals. It's also an AI, and AI gets things wrong. Here's an honest explanation of how it works and how to use it appropriately.

## What powers Copilot?

Atlas Copilot is powered by Claude, Anthropic's large language model. On top of Claude's general knowledge, Copilot has access to:

- The **Atlas HR knowledge base** — all Knowledge Hub articles, which cover HR best practices, policies, and processes
- **Country and jurisdiction context** — if you've set your primary country in workspace settings, Copilot uses this to contextualise its answers
- **Your conversation history** — within a session, Copilot remembers what you've discussed

## What Copilot is good at

**Practical HR guidance:** Copilot performs well on common HR scenarios — managing a PIP, structuring a disciplinary process, handling leave requests, writing offer letters. These are well-documented areas where the knowledge base and Claude's training combine effectively.

**Document drafting:** Copilot produces solid first drafts for letters, policies, and announcements. The drafts are structured, professional, and typically need only light editing.

**Explaining HR concepts:** If you need to understand what "constructive dismissal" means, how TUPE works in a UK context, or what a 30-60-90 day onboarding plan involves, Copilot explains these accurately and clearly.

**Summarising content:** Copilot retrieves and summarises Atlas HR knowledge articles accurately.

## Where Copilot has limitations

**Jurisdiction-specific legal detail:** Employment law varies enormously by country and changes frequently. Copilot's knowledge base is updated regularly, but law can change faster than training data. Never rely on Copilot as the sole source for a legal decision.

**Your specific facts:** Copilot doesn't know the specifics of your employees, your contracts, your internal policies, or your history with a particular situation. It gives general guidance — you apply it to your context.

**Recent changes:** Copilot's training data has a cutoff date. New legislation, regulatory guidance, or case law from the last few months may not be reflected.

**Complex or high-risk cases:** Discrimination claims, unfair dismissal cases, TUPE transfers, and redundancy processes involving large numbers of employees all carry significant legal risk. These warrant qualified legal advice, not just an AI assistant.

<Callout type="warning">
Atlas Copilot's advice is guidance, not legal advice. Before taking action on any high-risk HR matter — dismissal, disciplinary, discrimination complaint, or significant restructuring — consult a qualified employment lawyer or HR legal specialist in your jurisdiction.
</Callout>

## How to use Copilot well

Think of Copilot as a very knowledgeable colleague who has read everything in the Atlas HR knowledge base and a lot more besides. It's a great thinking partner, a fast drafter, and a useful sanity check. It is not a substitute for:

- Employment legal advice
- Your own professional judgment
- Knowledge of your specific employment contracts and internal policies

The best way to use Copilot is to:

1. Use it to get a starting point or structure your thinking
2. Check any specific legal claims against a reputable source (your employment lawyer, ACAS, or a government guidance page)
3. Apply your knowledge of the specific situation

## Can I trust Copilot's document drafts?

Copilot produces good first drafts. Before using any document drafted by Copilot, review it for:

- Accuracy to your specific situation
- Consistency with your internal policies and contracts
- Compliance with local law (especially for termination letters, disciplinary outcomes, and contracts)
- Your company's tone and style

A Copilot draft is a starting point, not a final document.
