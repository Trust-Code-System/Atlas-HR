Your email address is your Atlas HR login. Changing it requires a verification step to make sure the new address belongs to you. Here's how it works.

## How to change your email

<Steps>
1. Go to **Settings → Account**.
2. Under "Email address," click **Change email**.
3. Enter your new email address in the field provided.
4. Click **Send verification email**.
5. Open the email sent to your **new** address and click the confirmation link.
6. Once confirmed, your login email is updated immediately.
</Steps>

Your old email address stops working as a login as soon as you confirm the new one.

## What to expect during the change

- The verification email comes from **hello@atlashr.app** — check spam if you don't see it within two minutes.
- The confirmation link expires after **24 hours**. If it expires, just repeat the process.
- Until you confirm the new address, your old email still works to log in.
- If you signed up with Google, you'll need to manage your email through your Google account settings instead.

<Callout type="warning">
If you change your email and then can't access the new inbox (for example, a company email after leaving a job), contact us as soon as possible so we can help you regain access. We can manually verify identity through other means.
</Callout>

## What doesn't change when you update your email

- Your subscription and billing history stay the same
- Your documents and saved templates are unaffected
- Your workspace members and settings remain unchanged
- Your employee records and data stay intact

## Signed up with Google?

If you created your account via Google Sign-In, your email is tied to your Google account. Atlas HR can't change this directly — you'd need to either:

1. Update your Google account's primary email address (Google account settings), or
2. Create a separate Atlas HR account with a different email and contact support to migrate your data.

<Callout type="info">
For most users, switching Google accounts is more hassle than it's worth. If this situation applies to you, reach out and we'll find the simplest path forward.
</Callout>
