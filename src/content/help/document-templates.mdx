## What are templates?

Templates in Atlas HR are pre-built document structures that give you a starting point. Unlike AI tool generations (which create content from scratch based on your inputs), templates provide a fixed structure you fill in and customise.

Templates are useful for:

- Documents that need to look the same every time (e.g. a standard offer letter format)
- Content where the structure is mandated (e.g. statutory notices)
- Situations where you need a quick starting point without running an AI tool

## Finding templates

Browse all available templates at [/templates](/templates). You can filter by:

- Document type (contract, letter, policy, notice)
- Country (UK, US, EU, global, and more)
- Industry

Free plan users can access all templates marked **Free**. Pro and Team plan users can access all templates.

## Using a template

1. Open the template you want.
2. Click **Use template**.
3. Fill in the variable fields (names, dates, role details, etc.).
4. Click **Generate** — Atlas HR merges your inputs into the template.
5. Review the output, make any edits, and download or save.

## Saving a custom template

You can save your own edited documents as templates for reuse:

1. Open a saved document in your Library.
2. Click **Save as template**.
3. Give it a name and choose whether it's private (visible only to you) or shared with your workspace.

## Sharing templates with your workspace (Team plan)

Workspace admins can publish custom templates to all members. To do this, use **Save as template → Share with workspace** when saving. Members see shared templates in their template browser under **Your workspace**.

## Template vs AI tool

| | Template | AI Tool |
|---|---|---|
| Starting point | Fixed structure | Blank — AI drafts from scratch |
| Speed | Very fast | Fast |
| Customisation | You fill in fields | AI adapts to your inputs |
| Uses generation credit | Yes | Yes |
| Best for | Standardised documents | Complex, context-specific drafts |
