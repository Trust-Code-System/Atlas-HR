Atlas HR has a built-in reports module that gives you insight into your workforce without needing a separate analytics tool. Here's how to find and use it.

## Where to find reports

Go to **Reports** in the left sidebar. You'll see a list of available report types on the left.

Reports are available on the **Team plan and above**.

## Available reports

**Headcount report**
A snapshot of your current workforce. Shows total employees, broken down by department, location, employment type, and tenure bracket. Use this to answer "how many people do we have, and where?"

**Turnover report**
Shows how many employees left in a selected period, their tenure, and departure reason (if recorded during offboarding). Helps you track voluntary vs involuntary attrition.

**Leave report**
Shows leave taken across the workforce in a date range. Filter by leave type (annual, sick, parental, etc.), department, or individual employee. Useful for spotting high absence in a specific team.

**Compliance report**
Shows the document compliance status of your workforce. Filter by document type, status (green/amber/red), or department. Essential before an audit or HR review.

**Onboarding report**
Shows the onboarding task completion rate for recently joined employees. Flags employees who are overdue on tasks.

**Custom date range**
Most reports let you set a custom date range — useful for quarterly HR reviews or board reporting.

## How to run a report

<Steps>
1. Go to **Reports**.
2. Click the report you want from the list on the left.
3. Set your filters (date range, department, leave type, etc.).
4. Click **Generate report** or **Apply filters** — the report updates in real time.
5. Review the data in the table or chart view.
6. Click **Export CSV** to download the data.
</Steps>

## Using filters effectively

Most reports have at least these filters:

- **Date range** — select a preset (last 30 days, last quarter, this year) or enter custom dates
- **Department** — filter to one team or compare across departments
- **Employment type** — e.g. show only permanent employees, or only contractors

<Callout type="tip">
If you're preparing a report for leadership, generate it with a month-end date range (e.g. 1 April to 30 April) so the numbers are clean and consistent with your finance reporting periods.
</Callout>

## Exporting data

Every report has an **Export CSV** button. The export includes all rows matching your current filters. CSV files open in Excel, Google Sheets, or any data tool.

Exports are timestamped and include the filter settings used, so you can reproduce the exact report later.

<Callout type="info">
If you need formatted PDF reports for board presentations, use the CSV export and paste it into your existing report template. Atlas HR's CSV data is clean and straightforward to work with.
</Callout>

## Scheduled reports

You can set up automatic monthly email reports:

1. Run the report you want.
2. Click **Schedule report**.
3. Choose frequency (monthly is most common) and the recipient email(s).
4. Save.

You'll receive the report by email on the first working day of each month.
