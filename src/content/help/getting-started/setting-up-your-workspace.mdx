Your Atlas HR workspace is the hub for your organisation's HR activity. This guide walks you through creating it, adding your first employee, and understanding how the workspace is laid out.

## Creating your organisation

If you completed the onboarding wizard, your workspace is already created. If you skipped it, here's how to set it up now:

<Steps>
1. Go to **Settings → Workspace**.
2. Enter your **Organisation name** — this appears on generated documents and the employee portal.
3. Add your **primary country** — this sets the default jurisdiction for Copilot advice and generated documents.
4. Choose your **industry** — helps Atlas HR surface the most relevant templates.
5. Upload a **logo** (optional, but recommended if you share documents with employees or candidates).
6. Click **Save changes**.
</Steps>

## Overview of the workspace layout

The left sidebar is your main navigation. Here's what each section does:

- **Dashboard** — your activity feed, quick actions, and usage snapshot
- **Atlas Copilot** — your HR AI assistant for advice, drafting, and quick lookups
- **People** — the Mini-HRIS where you manage employee records, leave, and documents
- **Tools & Generators** — AI-powered document tools (job descriptions, offer letters, policies, etc.)
- **Templates** — ready-to-download HR document templates
- **Knowledge Hub** — in-depth HR articles and best practice guides
- **Reports** — headcount, leave, turnover, and compliance data
- **Settings** — workspace config, billing, members, and integrations

<Callout type="info">
The **People** section is only available on the Team plan and above. If you're on Free or Pro, you'll see a prompt to upgrade when you navigate there.
</Callout>

## Adding your first employee

Adding an employee creates their record in the Mini-HRIS. This is separate from inviting a team member (which gives someone an Atlas HR admin login).

<Steps>
1. Go to **People → Add employee**.
2. Enter their full name, job title, and start date.
3. Add their email address if you want to send them an employee portal invite later.
4. Fill in any additional fields you track (department, manager, employment type, etc.).
5. Click **Save employee**.
</Steps>

Their record is now live. You can add documents, set up onboarding tasks, and track their leave from their profile page.

## Customising what you track

Every organisation tracks different things. Atlas HR lets you configure custom fields for employee records:

1. Go to **Settings → Custom fields**.
2. Click **Add field** and choose the field type (text, date, dropdown, etc.).
3. Name the field and decide whether it's required.
4. Save — the field will appear on all employee records going forward.

<Callout type="tip">
Common custom fields include "Probation end date," "T-shirt size" (for welcome kits), "Emergency contact," and "Contract type." Add only what you'll actually use — cluttered records slow people down.
</Callout>

## What to do next

Once your workspace is set up, a good next step is to:

- Add your employees (or import them in bulk via CSV)
- Set up approval workflows if you need leave or document requests to go through a manager
- Invite your HR colleague as an Admin if you share the HR function
- Explore the Tools section to generate your first document
