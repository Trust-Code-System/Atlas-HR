Atlas HR's template library gives you ready-to-use HR documents that you can customise and download without spending a generation. Here's how to find, customise, and download a template.

## Finding a template

Go to **Templates** in the left sidebar. You can browse by category:

- Employment contracts
- Offer letters
- HR policies
- Job descriptions
- Performance letters
- Disciplinary documents
- Onboarding documents
- Reference letters

Use the search bar to find a specific document type. Templates are tagged by country where relevant (e.g. "UK Employment Contract" vs "Nigeria Employment Contract").

## Previewing a template

Click any template to see a full preview before downloading. The preview shows the complete document with placeholder text (shown in {{double curly braces}}) where you'll need to fill in specific details.

## Customising before downloading

Templates can be customised directly in Atlas HR before you download:

<Steps>
1. Open the template preview.
2. Click **Customise template**.
3. Fill in the fields on the left panel — these map to the placeholders in the document.
4. The preview on the right updates as you type.
5. You can also edit the document body directly by clicking in the preview.
6. When you're happy, click **Download**.
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip">
If you have specific clauses or sections your organisation always uses, add them in the "Additional clauses" field if available, or click directly in the document body to add them. These won't be overwritten when you update the form fields.
</Callout>

## Download formats

**DOCX (Microsoft Word)**
Available on all plans. Best for documents you'll share with a hiring manager, employee, or external party who may want to make further edits. Compatible with Word, Google Docs, LibreOffice, and most ATS platforms.

**PDF**
Available on Pro and above. Best for final documents that should not be edited — for example, an offer letter or signed contract you're archiving. If you're on Free, export from Google Docs or Word after editing.

## Saving a customised template

On Pro and above, you can save your customised version as a personal template:

1. After customising, click **Save as my template** instead of download.
2. Give it a name (e.g. "Our standard UK offer letter").
3. It appears in **My Templates** for future use.

This is particularly useful if you've added company-specific clauses you want in every document of that type.

## Template vs Generator — which should I use?

| Use a **template** when... | Use the **generator** when... |
|---|---|
| You know exactly what structure you want | You want AI to draft the content for you |
| You need a document fast without spending a generation | You have a blank page and need a first draft |
| You want consistent formatting across documents | The document needs to be tailored to specific inputs |

<Callout type="info">
Templates are available even on the Free plan — you can download and edit them without using a generation. AI generation is what's limited on Free, not access to the template library.
</Callout>
