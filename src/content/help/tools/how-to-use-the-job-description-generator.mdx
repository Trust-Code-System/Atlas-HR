The Job Description Generator is one of Atlas HR's most-used tools. In about 60 seconds of input, it produces a structured, ready-to-edit job description. Here's how to get the best out of it.

## Finding the tool

Go to **Tools → Job Description Generator** from the left sidebar. If you're on mobile, tap the menu icon and then Tools.

## Filling in the form

The generator has several input fields. Here's what each one affects in the output:

**Job title**
The most important field. Be specific — "HR Business Partner (EMEA)" produces a much better output than "HR person." The title shapes the entire document structure.

**Company name**
Appears in the opening paragraph and the "About us" section. Use your actual company name, not a placeholder.

**Company description (2–3 sentences)**
Gives the generator context about your organisation's size, stage, and industry. A sentence like "We're a 120-person logistics startup in Lagos, Series B funded, expanding into East Africa" produces output that's far more relevant than a generic description.

**Location and working pattern**
Specify whether the role is office-based, hybrid, or remote, and where. This directly affects the language around working arrangements in the output.

**Seniority level**
Choose from Junior, Mid-level, Senior, or Leadership. This shapes the responsibility complexity, years of experience suggested, and tone.

**Key responsibilities (bullet points)**
Enter the main things this person will own. 4–6 bullets work well. The generator expands these into full responsibility descriptions with scope and context.

**Must-have requirements**
The non-negotiables. Keep to 4–6. The generator treats these as hard requirements and lists them separately from nice-to-haves.

**Nice-to-have requirements**
Skills or experience that would be useful but aren't essential.

**Salary range**
If you include this, it appears in the compensation section. We strongly recommend including it — transparency improves candidate quality.

<Callout type="tip">
The more context you give in the "Company description" and "Key responsibilities" fields, the less editing the output will need. Sparse inputs produce generic outputs.
</Callout>

## How the output works

When you click **Generate**, you'll see the job description appear word by word on screen. This is **streaming output** — the text is being generated in real time, not loaded from a file. It takes 15–45 seconds depending on the length.

**Don't close the tab or navigate away while it's streaming.** If the connection drops mid-stream, you'll get a partial document. Just regenerate — it counts as one generation.

## Editing the output

Once the document finishes generating:

- Click anywhere in the text to edit it directly
- The editor supports basic formatting (bold, bullet points, headings)
- Use the **Regenerate section** button next to any section to rewrite just that part

## Downloading the document

Click **Download DOCX** to save the job description as a Microsoft Word file. This opens the full download options:

- **DOCX** — compatible with Word, Google Docs, and LibreOffice
- **PDF** — a clean, print-ready version (available on Pro plan)
- **Copy to clipboard** — pastes plain text into your ATS or email

<Callout type="info">
DOCX is available on all plans. PDF download is a Pro plan feature. If you need PDF on Free, copy the text and paste it into Google Docs, then export to PDF from there.
</Callout>

## Saving to your library

On Pro and above, click **Save to library** to store the document in your document library. From there you can find it later, organise it in folders, and share it with workspace members.
