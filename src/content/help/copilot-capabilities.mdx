## What Atlas Copilot does

Atlas Copilot is an AI assistant built specifically for HR work. You can use it to:

**Draft content**
- Write or redraft HR policies
- Draft disciplinary or performance improvement letters
- Create job descriptions and interview questions
- Write manager talking points for difficult conversations

**Answer HR questions**
- Explain employment law concepts (redundancy, TUPE, constructive dismissal, etc.)
- Describe how processes work (grievance procedures, PILON, garden leave)
- Compare approaches (e.g. "What's the difference between a PIP and a managed exit?")

**Review and advise**
- Spot gaps in a policy you paste in
- Suggest improvements to an offer letter or contract clause
- Walk you through a process step by step

**Explain documents**
- Summarise a contract in plain English
- Explain what a statutory notice means in practice

## What to verify

Copilot is knowledgeable but not infallible. Always verify advice before acting on it when:

- The question involves **specific legal risk** (tribunal claims, discrimination, TUPE)
- The answer depends on **recent legislation changes** (Copilot's training has a knowledge cutoff)
- You're relying on it for **jurisdiction-specific advice** outside the UK, US, or EU

For legal-sensitive decisions, treat Copilot as a research aid and consult a qualified employment lawyer.

## Tips for better results

- **Be specific**: "Write a 3-month PIP for a sales manager missing targets by 20%" gets better results than "write a PIP".
- **Provide context**: Paste in the relevant contract clause or policy text when asking about a specific situation.
- **Iterate**: Follow up with "make it shorter", "use a warmer tone", or "add a section on expected outcomes".
- **Use it for first drafts**: Copilot excels at generating starting points you then refine.

## Copilot vs the Tools

**Atlas Copilot** is conversational — back-and-forth dialogue, open-ended questions, free-form drafting.

**AI Tools** (offer letter generator, performance review tool, etc.) are structured forms that take specific inputs and produce a formatted document in one step. They're faster for standard documents.

Use Copilot when the situation is nuanced or you want to explore options. Use a Tool when you know exactly what document you need.
