This article is written for **employees** who need to upload documents to their HR portal. Examples include your passport, right to work documentation, signed contract, or professional certifications.

## Where to upload documents

<Steps>
1. Log in to your employee portal.
2. Click **Documents** in the navigation menu.
3. You'll see two sections: "Required documents" (what HR has asked you to provide) and "My documents" (anything you've uploaded yourself).
</Steps>

## Uploading a required document

When HR has requested a specific document, it appears in the "Required documents" section with a status of "Missing" or "Expired."

<Steps>
1. Click the document requirement (e.g. "Passport copy" or "Right to work").
2. Click **Upload document**.
3. Select the file from your device. Supported formats: PDF, JPG, PNG, DOCX. Maximum file size: 20MB.
4. If the document has an expiry date (like a visa or certification), enter it in the **Expiry date** field.
5. Click **Save**.
</Steps>

Your document is uploaded and the status changes to **Pending review**.

<Callout type="info">
The expiry date field is important for documents like visas, work permits, and professional licences. Atlas HR uses this date to send you renewal reminders before the document expires.
</Callout>

## What "Pending review" means

After you upload, an HR administrator needs to review and approve your document. This is a quick process for your HR team — typically done within one to two business days.

You'll receive an email when your document is:
- **Approved** — the document is accepted and on file. The status goes green.
- **Rejected** — the HR team has requested a different version or format. The email will include a note explaining what's needed. You can then re-upload.

<Callout type="warning">
If your document is rejected, please re-upload as soon as possible. Documents marked as required by HR can affect your employment record if they remain missing or expired for too long.
</Callout>

## Uploading a document not on the required list

You can also upload documents proactively — for example, a training certificate you've completed or an updated emergency contact form.

1. In the Documents section, scroll to **My documents**.
2. Click **Upload document**.
3. Select the file and choose a document type from the dropdown.
4. Add an expiry date if relevant.
5. Save.

These uploads go to your HR record and are visible to your HR team. They don't require HR to request them first.

## What file formats are accepted?

- PDF (recommended for signed documents)
- JPG or PNG (for scanned IDs, photographs)
- DOCX (for editable documents, though PDF is preferred for final signed versions)

Files must be under 20MB. If your file is larger, compress it or scan at a lower resolution.

## My document is showing as expired

If an expiry date has passed, your document status will show as "Expired" in red. Upload the renewed version using the same upload steps above — choose the same document type and enter the new expiry date.
