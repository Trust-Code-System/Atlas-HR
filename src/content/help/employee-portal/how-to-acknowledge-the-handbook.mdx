This article is written for **employees** who have been asked to acknowledge the employee handbook or a company policy document through their Atlas HR portal.

## What is a handbook acknowledgement?

When your employer shares a handbook or policy document and wants a record that you've read it, they can assign an acknowledgement task through Atlas HR. Completing this task creates a timestamped record in the HR system confirming that you acknowledged the document on a specific date.

This is common for employee handbooks, code of conduct documents, data protection policies, and health and safety guidelines.

## Finding the acknowledgement task

<Steps>
1. Log in to your employee portal.
2. Click **Tasks** in the navigation.
3. You'll see any outstanding tasks assigned to you. Look for a task titled something like "Acknowledge employee handbook" or "Review and confirm code of conduct."
4. Click on the task to open it.
</Steps>

<Callout type="info">
If you've just joined and completed onboarding, handbook acknowledgement is often one of the first tasks on your list. Tasks are ordered by due date — overdue tasks appear at the top in red.
</Callout>

## Reading the document

When you open the acknowledgement task, you'll see:

- A description of what you're being asked to acknowledge
- A button to **View document** — this opens the handbook or policy in a PDF viewer within the portal

Take the time to read the document properly. The acknowledgement is a confirmation that you've had the opportunity to read and understand the content.

## Acknowledging the document

<Steps>
1. After reading the document, click the back arrow or close the viewer to return to the task.
2. You'll see an **Acknowledge** button and a statement such as: "I confirm I have read and understood the [Document Name] dated [Date]."
3. Click **Acknowledge**.
4. The task status changes to "Completed" and the timestamp is recorded.
</Steps>

You'll receive a confirmation notification, and the task will move from "Outstanding" to "Completed" in your tasks list.

<Callout type="warning">
Only click Acknowledge if you have genuinely read the document. If you have questions about any section, contact your manager or HR team **before** acknowledging — not after.
</Callout>

## What if I disagree with something in the handbook?

If a policy raises concerns for you, reach out to your HR team before acknowledging. They can clarify the intent, discuss any individual circumstances, or escalate to the appropriate person. You don't need to acknowledge a document you haven't had a chance to understand.

## What does my HR team see?

Your employer's HR system will show:

- Your name
- The document you acknowledged
- The exact date and time you clicked Acknowledge

This record is retained as part of your employment record for as long as your employer stores HR data.

## I can't find the task

If your HR team told you there's a handbook acknowledgement to complete but you can't see it in Tasks:

1. Check that you're logged into the correct portal (your employer may have multiple locations).
2. Refresh the page.
3. Contact your HR manager — they can check if the task was assigned correctly and resend if needed.
