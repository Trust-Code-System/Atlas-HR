## Overview

The Mini-HRIS includes a leave management module that lets employees request leave and managers approve or decline requests — all within Atlas HR. Balances are tracked automatically.

## Leave types

Atlas HR comes with standard leave types pre-configured:

- Annual leave
- Sick leave
- Parental leave (maternity / paternity / shared)
- Compassionate leave
- Unpaid leave
- Other (custom reason)

Workspace admins can add custom leave types from **Settings → Leave types**.

## Configuring annual leave entitlement

Set the default annual leave entitlement for your organisation at **Settings → Leave → Annual entitlement**. Individual employee entitlements can be overridden on each employee's profile if they differ from the default.

## How employees request leave

If employees have access to the employee portal:

1. Employee logs in to the portal.
2. Clicks **Request leave**.
3. Selects the leave type, start date, end date, and adds a note.
4. Submits the request.

If employees don't have portal access, an HR admin can submit leave requests on their behalf from the employee's profile.

## Approving or declining leave

Managers and admins receive an in-app notification and an email when a leave request is submitted.

To approve or decline:

1. Go to **People → Leave requests**, or click the notification link.
2. Review the request and the employee's remaining balance.
3. Click **Approve** or **Decline** (with an optional reason).

The employee is notified by email of the decision.

## Viewing leave balances

See an employee's leave balance on their profile under **Leave**. The balance shows:

- Total entitlement for the year
- Days taken
- Days approved/pending
- Days remaining

## Leave calendar

View the whole team's approved leave on the **Leave calendar** (People → Leave → Calendar). This helps identify coverage gaps before approving new requests.

## Carrying over leave

Configure leave carryover rules at **Settings → Leave → Carryover**. Options include no carryover, unlimited carryover, and capped carryover (e.g. max 5 days).

Carryover is applied automatically at the start of the new leave year.
